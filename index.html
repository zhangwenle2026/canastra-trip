Chapter Two

Destaques

01

Casca d'Anta

A 186-meter waterfall marking the birthplace of the São Francisco River. Brazil's third-longest river begins here as a thin stream atop the plateau before plunging in a spectacular free-fall into the valley below. The upper viewpoint offers panoramic vistas across the cerrado; the lower trail leads to the thundering base pool.

02

Chapadão da Canastra

A 1,400-meter altitude plateau forming the park's spine. The cerrado ecosystem here hosts unique flora and fauna adapted to high-altitude grasslands, perpetual winds, and dramatic temperature swings. Driving across feels like traversing another planet — endless sky meeting golden grass at every horizon.

03

Queijo Canastra

A 200-year artisanal cheese tradition recognized as Brazilian cultural heritage by IPHAN. Made from raw milk with natural pingo starter culture, each wheel tells the story of its terroir. From fresh and creamy at 22 days to aged, crystalline, and intensely savory at one year — an essential tasting journey.

04

Complexo do Claro

A multi-level waterfall complex near Delfinópolis. Several cascades flow over ancient red sandstone into natural swimming pools of startling clarity. Each level reveals new formations, rock slides, and emerald pools. One of the region's finest bathing spots — bring time, not just a towel.

05

Lago de Furnas

Known as the "Mar de Minas" — the Sea of Minas — this vast reservoir stretches across 1,440 square kilometers. Golden sunsets reflect off calm waters while surrounding hills frame perfect evening tableaux. A contemplative counterpoint to the park's wild energy.

06

Café Mineiro

Minas Gerais produces over half of Brazil's coffee. The Canastra region's altitude, volcanic soil, and temperature differential yield exceptional single-origin beans — notes of dark chocolate, citrus, and brown sugar. Visit a family farm, see the process from cherry to cup, and understand why terroir matters.

Chapter Three

Notas do Viajante

Transportation

The route follows BR-381 (Fernão Dias) from São Paulo to MG-050, then local roads to São João Batista do Glória. Total round trip approximately 880km. Return via SP-330 (Anhanguera) — a well-maintained toll highway.

  • Roads within Serra da Canastra are unpaved gravel — well-maintained but dusty in dry season
  • 4WD/AWD strongly recommended for the Chapadão crossing
  • Last gas station before the park: São João Batista do Glória or São Roque de Minas
  • Delfinópolis area: paved main roads, but waterfall access roads are often dirt
  • Download offline maps — phone signal is minimal to nonexistent inside the park

Accommodation

  • Night 1: Casa Serra da Canastra — Pousada in São João Batista do Glória, rustic-chic with mountain views
  • Nights 2–3: Lakeside Airbnb in São José da Barra — private house overlooking Furnas Reservoir with deck and BBQ
  • Book well in advance for July (dry season coincides with Brazilian winter school holidays)
  • Bring warm layers — nights at altitude can drop to 8–12 degrees Celsius

Best Season

April to October (dry season) is ideal. Clear skies, mild temperatures, waterfalls flowing strong from late rainy season reserves. July offers the best combination of dry weather and full water volume. Avoid December–March when heavy rains make park roads impassable and waterfalls dangerously strong.

Packing

  • Hiking boots or sturdy trail shoes — waterfall trails are slippery
  • Swimwear and quick-dry towel — natural pools await at every turn
  • Sunscreen SPF50+ and wide-brim hat — the plateau offers no shade
  • Light rain jacket — morning mist can soak through layers
  • Warm fleece for evenings — altitude cold surprises
  • Binoculars for wildlife spotting from distance
  • Refillable water bottles, 2L minimum per person for park days
  • Snacks and energy bars for the Chapadão crossing
  • Cash — small towns may not accept cards universally

Safety

  • Gravel roads: maintain 40–60km/h, watch for loose stones and wildlife
  • Wildlife: observe from distance, never feed or approach maned wolves
  • Sun: the plateau at 1,400m has intense UV, even on overcast days
  • Water: check current strength before swimming near waterfalls
  • Park hours: gates typically open 08:00–18:00, plan entry/exit accordingly
  • Emergency: nearest hospital is in Passos (90km from the park)
