Chapter 02

六大亮点

01

Casca d'Anta

A 186-meter waterfall marking the source of the São Francisco River. Brazil's third-longest river begins here as a thin stream before plunging off the plateau in a spectacular free-fall. The upper viewpoint offers panoramic vistas; the lower trail leads to the base pool.

02

Chapadão da Canastra

A 1400-meter altitude plateau stretching across the park's spine. The cerrado ecosystem here hosts unique flora and fauna adapted to high-altitude grasslands. Driving across feels like traversing another planet — endless sky meeting golden grass.

03

Queijo Canastra

A 200-year artisanal cheese tradition recognized as Brazilian cultural heritage by IPHAN. Made from raw milk with natural pingo starter culture, each wheel tells the story of its terroir. From fresh and creamy to aged and crystalline — an essential tasting experience.

04

Complexo do Claro

A multi-level waterfall complex near Delfinópolis. Several cascades flow over red sandstone into natural swimming pools of startling clarity. Each level reveals new formations, slides, and pools. One of the region's finest bathing spots.

05

Lago de Furnas

Known as the "Sea of Minas," this vast reservoir stretches across 1,440 square kilometers. Golden sunsets reflect off calm waters while the surrounding hills frame perfect evening scenes. A contemplative counterpoint to the park's wild energy.

06

Café Mineiro

Minas Gerais produces over half of Brazil's coffee. The Canastra region's altitude, climate, and volcanic soil yield exceptional single-origin beans. Notes of dark chocolate, citrus, and caramel. Visit a family farm, see the process from cherry to cup.

Chapter 03

旅行者手记

Transportation

The main route follows BR-381 (Fernão Dias) from São Paulo to MG-050, then local roads to São João Batista do Glória. Total round trip approximately 880km.

  • Roads within Serra da Canastra are unpaved gravel — well-maintained but dusty in dry season
  • 4WD/AWD strongly recommended for the Chapadão crossing
  • Last gas station before the park: São João Batista do Glória or São Roque de Minas
  • Delfinópolis area roads are paved but some waterfall access roads are dirt
  • SP-330 (Anhanguera) for the return is a well-maintained toll highway

Accommodation

  • Night 1: Casa Serra da Canastra — Pousada in São João Batista do Glória, rustic-chic style with mountain views
  • Nights 2-3: Lakeside Airbnb near Delfinópolis — Private house overlooking Furnas Reservoir with deck and BBQ area
  • Book well in advance for July (dry season / Brazilian winter holidays)
  • Bring warm layers — nights at altitude can drop to 8-12 degrees Celsius

Best Season

April to October (dry season) is ideal. Clear skies, mild temperatures, waterfalls flowing strong from late rainy season reserves. July offers the best combination of dry weather and full water volume. Avoid December-March when heavy rains can make park roads impassable and waterfalls dangerously strong.

Packing List

  • Hiking boots or sturdy trail shoes (waterfall trails are slippery)
  • Swimwear and quick-dry towel (natural pools everywhere)
  • Sunscreen SPF50+ and wide-brim hat (plateau has no shade)
  • Light rain jacket (morning mist can be heavy)
  • Warm fleece for evenings (altitude cold)
  • Binoculars for wildlife spotting
  • Refillable water bottles (2L minimum per person for park days)
  • Snacks and energy bars for the park crossing
  • Cash (small towns may not accept cards everywhere)
  • Camera with wide-angle lens for the landscapes

Safety Tips

  • Gravel roads: maintain 40-60km/h, watch for loose stones and wildlife crossing
  • Wildlife: observe from distance, never feed or approach maned wolves or anteaters
  • Sun protection: the plateau at 1400m has intense UV, even on cloudy days
  • Water safety: check current strength before swimming near waterfalls
  • Phone signal: limited to non-existent inside the park; download offline maps
  • Park hours: gates typically open 08:00-18:00; plan accordingly
